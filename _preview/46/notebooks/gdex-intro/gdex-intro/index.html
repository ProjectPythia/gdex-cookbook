Overview¶

NSF NCAR’s Geoscience Data Exchange (GDEX) is the long-term, trusted home for geoscience data at the National Center for Atmospheric Research (NCAR), funded by the National Science Foundation (NSF). GDEX bridges the gap between data producers and the broader scientific community by providing curated, openly accessible datasets alongside the tools and services that can help enable scientific insight.

Whether you are looking to download terabytes of reanalysis data, stream a climate model output directly into a Python workflow, or archive your own research dataset with a citable DOI, GDEX is built to support the full lifecycle of geoscience data.

Visit GDEX at gdex.ucar.edu

In this Cookbook you will learn:¶

  1. The mission and purpose of GDEX

  2. The breadth of data collections available

  3. Key services and tools for data access and analysis

  4. How to submit your own data to GDEX

  5. How GDEX enables reproducible, open science

Mission and Purpose¶

GDEX exists to make geoscience data findable, accessible, interoperable, and reusable (FAIR) to the NCAR scientists and the broader community.

Open Science¶

GDEX is committed to open science. Currently, all datasets are freely accessible to anyone, anywhere in the world without institutional or technical barriers. Additionally, data services are continuously being developed and expanded to minimize friction with obtaining and interacting with data.

Supporting the Full Data Lifecycle¶

GDEX supports data from initial archival through active use in research workflows:

  • Ingest & Curation: Data submitted to GDEX is reviewed, documented, and formatted to community standards

  • Discovery: Rich metadata and search tools help users find the right dataset quickly

  • Access: Multiple access pathways — from bulk download to cloud streaming

  • Citation: Persistent identifiers (DOIs) ensure datasets are citable and contributions are credited

  • Preservation: GDEX provides long-term stewardship and data backups

  • Stewardship: Science-educated staff assist users in solving problems and forwarding science.

Note

All GDEX datasets are freely accessible — no institutional login is required to browse, search, or download public datasets.

Data Collections¶

GDEX hosts thousands of datasets spanning the full breadth of the geosciences. Collections range from petabytes of global reanalysis output to niche observational records from field campaigns.

Ocean and Earth System Data¶

  • Oceanographic: ICOADS — the International Comprehensive Ocean-Atmosphere Data Set, spanning centuries of marine surface observations

  • Land surface: Soil moisture, vegetation, and land-use datasets supporting land-atmosphere interaction research

  • Cryosphere: Sea ice concentration, snow cover, and ice sheet data

Data Submission¶

GDEX accepts data submissions from the broader scientific community. Archiving your data with GDEX is a way to ensure it reaches the widest possible audience and remains accessible for the long term.

Who Can Submit?¶

Data submission is open to:

  • NSF NCAR scientists and collaborators conducting funded research

  • University researchers and UCAR member institutions

  • Project teams with NSF or other federally funded grants that require a data management plan

If you are unsure whether your dataset is a good fit, contact the GDEX team at datahelp@ucar.edu.

What Types of Data Are Accepted?¶

GDEX prioritizes datasets that:

  • Are of broad community interest for atmospheric, oceanic, or related geoscience research

  • Come with sufficient documentation and metadata to make the data reusable

  • Are in community-standard formats (or can be converted to them)

  • Represent unique or irreplaceable observational records or model output

The Submission Process¶

  1. Contact the GDEX team — Submit your data and discuss its suitability with the GDEX team

  2. Prepare your data — Work with the GDEX team to ensure your data and metadata meet community standards

  3. Transfer your data — GDEX supports transfer via Globus, HTTPS, or other methods depending on data volume

  4. Curation and review — The GDEX team reviews, documents, and organizes your dataset

  5. Publication and DOI — Your dataset is published with a persistent DOI, making it citable in publications

Benefits of Archiving with GDEX¶

  • Your dataset receives a citable DOI that can be included in publications

  • Long-term preservation and backups by a trusted, CoreTrustSeal certified repository

  • Broad discoverability through GDEX search and external indexes (e.g., Google Dataset Search)

  • Compliance with NSF and journal data sharing requirements

  • Access to a variety of value added services

  • Your data becomes part of a collection used by researchers worldwide

Tip

Contact datahelp@ucar.edu before investing significant effort in data preparation. The GDEX team can confirm your dataset is a good fit and advise on format and metadata requirements.

How GDEX Enables Science¶

GDEX is more than a file repository — it is infrastructure that enables scientific discovery at scale.

Reproducible Research¶

By providing stable, versioned, citable datasets, GDEX makes it possible to reproduce published analyses years after the fact. When a paper cites a GDEX dataset DOI, any researcher in the world can access the exact same data used in that study.

Lowering Barriers to Large-Scale Analysis¶

Some of the most scientifically important datasets are also the largest. ERA5 global reanalysis alone spans petabytes. GDEX removes the logistical barriers to working with these datasets by:

  • Hosting data on infrastructure optimized for high-throughput access

  • Providing cloud-optimized formats (Zarr, Kerchunk) that enable streaming analysis without full downloads

  • Supporting compute-proximate workflows on NSF NCAR HPC systems

Enabling Multi-Dataset Science¶

Because GDEX curates data to common standards, it becomes easier to combine datasets — for example, comparing reanalysis products or merging model output with observations. Consistent metadata and file formats reduce the data wrangling burden and let researchers focus on science.

Supporting Data-Driven Discovery¶

The scale and diversity of GDEX collections make them well-suited for emerging methods in geoscience:

  • Machine learning applications that require large training datasets

  • Long-term trend analysis using multi-decadal observational records

  • Model evaluation comparing simulations against observational benchmarks

  • Climate impact studies combining atmospheric, oceanic, and land surface data

Summary¶

NSF NCAR’s Geoscience Data Exchange (GDEX) is a trusted, long-term home for geoscience data — one that serves the research community both as a data archive and as an active platform for scientific discovery.

Key takeaways:

  • GDEX hosts thousands of datasets spanning atmospheric, oceanic, and Earth system science, from century-long observational records to petabyte-scale reanalysis products

  • Multiple access pathways — web download, Globus, OPeNDAP, and cloud streaming — ensure data is reachable wherever researchers work

  • Data submission is open to the community: archiving your data with GDEX provides a citable DOI, long-term preservation, and broad discoverability

  • GDEX enables science by lowering barriers to large-scale data access, supporting reproducibility, and providing infrastructure for modern data-driven workflows

The notebooks in this cookbook demonstrate hands-on use of GDEX data, from basic access patterns to cloud-native analysis at scale.

Getting Help¶

The GDEX team provides direct support for data access, discovery, and submission questions.

Data Help Desk¶

The primary support channel is the GDEX Data Help Desk:

  • Email: datahelp@ucar.edu

  • Response time: typically 1–2 business days

  • Best for: data access problems, subsetting questions, format guidance, submission inquiries, and anything not covered by documentation

When emailing, include the dataset identifier (e.g., ds083.2) and a description of what you were trying to do — this significantly speeds up the response.
