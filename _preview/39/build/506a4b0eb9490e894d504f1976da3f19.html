    JRA-3Q contains errors in sea ice cover for the period June 1985 to April 2013. Areas within approximately 250 km of the sea ice margin (fractional coverage of 0.1 or above) in both northern and southern hemispheres where ice cover should be 0.0 instead average roughly 0.15. Notable associated effects include increased surface upward short-wave radiation flux, reduced surface upward long-wave radiation flux, reduced sensible heat flux, reduced latent heat flux, reduced 2 meter temperature, and increased 2 meter relative humidity.
